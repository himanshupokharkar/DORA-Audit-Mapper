THE PROMPT (copy everything below this line)
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Please convert the following DORA mapping output into an Excel testing
workbook. Build the file using Python and openpyxl, and deliver it as
a downloadable Excel file.

The workbook must have exactly four sheets in this order:

───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
SHEET 1 — "1. Audit Context"
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Two columns (A: label, B: value). Column A width 30, Column B width 80.

Title row (row 1): "DORA Audit Testing Workbook" — merged across A:B,
dark blue fill (hex 305496), white bold Arial 16pt, height 30.

Pre-populate the following rows from the mapping's Audit Context:
  - Audit topic
  - Entity type
  - Audit objective
  - Scope exclusions applied

Then include these fillable rows (value column left blank, highlighted
yellow fill hex FFF2CC):
  - (blank separator row)
  - Engagement details  (section label, no yellow fill)
  - Audit lead
  - Audit team members
  - Reviewer
  - Audit period (start)
  - Audit period (end)
  - Fieldwork start date
  - Fieldwork completion date
  - (blank separator row)
  - Source files loaded  (pre-populate: "Main DORA (Reg 2022/2554);
    RTS ICT-RMF (Reg 2024/1774); RTS TLPT (Reg 2025/1190); RTS
    Incident Classification (Reg 2024/1772); RTS Incident Reporting
    (Reg 2025/301)")
  - (blank separator row)
  - How to use this workbook  (label, bold, no fill)

The "How to use this workbook" value cell must contain the following
eight numbered steps verbatim:

1.  START WITH AUDIT CONTEXT (this sheet)
    Complete the engagement details — audit lead, team members,
    reviewer, audit period and fieldwork dates. These fields are
    highlighted in yellow and should be filled in before fieldwork
    begins.

2.  REVIEW THE TESTING PROGRAMME (Sheet 2)
    Each row represents one DORA regulatory article or sub-provision
    in scope for this audit. Rows are colour-coded by section:
    • Green = Section 1 (Direct obligations — must be tested without
      preconditions)
    • Amber = Section 2 (Conditional obligations — evaluate the trigger
      condition before testing)
    • Navy  = Section 4 (Awareness items — review context but no direct
      testing obligation)

3.  DESIGN YOUR TESTING PROCEDURES
    For each row, complete the 'Testing Procedure' column with the
    specific steps you will take to test the obligation. Use the
    'Obligation Summary' and 'Full Article Text' columns as your
    reference. For Section 2 rows, confirm the trigger condition is
    met before proceeding.

4.  RECORD EVIDENCE
    As fieldwork progresses, complete 'Evidence Requested' (what you
    asked for), 'Evidence Obtained' (what was received and its
    reference), 'Sample Size' (how many items tested), and 'Testing
    Date' and 'Tester' columns.

5.  RECORD YOUR TESTING RESULT
    Use the dropdown in the 'Testing Result' column to record the
    outcome for each row:
    • Compliant       — control operates as required
    • Non-compliant   — control is absent or failed
    • Partial         — control partially operates but has gaps
    • N/A             — provision confirmed not applicable after
                        trigger evaluation
    • Not tested      — in scope but not tested in this cycle

6.  RECORD OBSERVATIONS AND FOLLOW-UPS
    Use the 'Observations / Findings' column to document any findings,
    exceptions or notes. Set 'Follow-up Required' to Yes where further
    action is needed and describe the follow-up in the Observations
    column.

7.  REVIEW EXCLUDED ITEMS (Sheet 4)
    Sheet 4 lists all DORA provisions that were retrieved during the
    regulatory mapping but deliberately excluded from scope, with
    reasons. Review this sheet to confirm you agree with the exclusion
    rationale before signing off the audit scope.

8.  COVERAGE ASSESSMENT (Sheet 3)
    Sheet 3 contains the regulatory coverage assessment, including
    provisions not yet loaded in the knowledge base, confidence level,
    and specific compliance-review testing considerations. Review this
    before finalising your audit programme.

Row height for the How to use row: 320. Freeze pane at A2.

───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
SHEET 2 — "2. Testing Programme"
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This is the main audit working sheet. One row per article cited in
Sections 1, 2, and 4 of the mapping. Do NOT include Section 3 or
Section 5 items as rows.

COLUMNS (exactly in this order, A through W):

  A  — Row #  (sequential integer, 1 upwards)
  B  — Section  (e.g. "Section 1", "Section 2", "Section 4")
  C  — Article Reference  (e.g. "Art.10(1)", "Art.19(1)(a)")
  D  — Source Document  (e.g. "Main DORA", "RTS Incident Classification")
  E  — Article Title  (from the mapping or Article Text Appendix)
  F  — Sub-Topic  (from the mapping)
  G  — Obligation Type  (Direct / Conditional / Permissive / Indirect)
  H  — Entity Scope  (from the mapping)
  I  — Trigger Condition  (for Section 2 rows; blank for Section 1)
  J  — Obligation Summary  (from the mapping)
  K  — Full Article Text  (verbatim paragraph text from the Article
       Text Appendix; if not in Appendix use the obligation summary)
  L  — Audit Action / Why Applicable  (from the mapping)
  M  — Expanded by  (from the mapping's Expanded by column)
  N  — Testing Procedure  (blank — auditor fills)
  O  — Sample Size  (blank — auditor fills)
  P  — Evidence Requested  (blank — auditor fills)
  Q  — Evidence Obtained (Y/N + ref)  (blank — auditor fills)
  R  — Testing Date  (blank — auditor fills)
  S  — Tester  (blank — auditor fills)
  T  — Testing Result  (blank with dropdown: Compliant / Non-compliant
       / Partial / N/A / Not tested)
  U  — Observations / Findings  (blank — auditor fills)
  V  — Follow-up Required  (blank with dropdown: Yes / No)
  W  — Cross-References  (blank — auditor fills)

COLUMN WIDTHS:
  A=7, B=10, C=18, D=22, E=25, F=30, G=14, H=25, I=35, J=50,
  K=80, L=40, M=35, N=40, O=12, P=30, Q=25, R=14, S=18,
  T=18, U=40, V=14, W=30

HEADER ROW formatting:
  - Font: Arial 11pt bold white
  - Fill: dark blue hex 305496
  - Alignment: centre horizontal, centre vertical, wrap text
  - Apply a thin light grey border (hex CCCCCC) to all header cells

DATA ROW formatting:
  - Font: Arial 10pt
  - Alignment: wrap text, top vertical, left horizontal
  - Thin light grey border on all cells
  - Row height: 90
  - Section colour coding applied to column B only:
      Section 1 → green fill hex 1F6F3E, white bold text
      Section 2 → amber fill hex C77700, white bold text
      Section 4 → navy fill hex 1F4E78, white bold text
      Column B text alignment: centre horizontal, centre vertical

DATA VALIDATION:
  - Column T (Testing Result): dropdown list
    "Compliant,Non-compliant,Partial,N/A,Not tested"
  - Column V (Follow-up Required): dropdown list "Yes,No"
  - Apply validation to all data rows (row 2 to last data row)

FREEZE PANES at D2 (freeze row 1 header and columns A-C).

ROW ORDER: All Section 1 rows first, then all Section 2 rows, then
all Section 4 rows. Within each section, preserve the order they
appear in the mapping output.

───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
SHEET 3 — "3. Coverage Assessment"
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Two columns (A: label, B: value). Column A width 35, Column B width 70.

Title row: "Coverage Assessment" — merged A:B, purple fill hex 5B2C6F,
white bold Arial 16pt, height 30.

Reproduce the full Coverage Assessment from the mapping output:
  - Total provisions retrieved (number)
  - Section 1 — Directly applicable (number)
  - Section 2 — Conditionally applicable (number)
  - Section 3 — Cross-referenced RTS/ITS (number)
  - Section 4 — Indirect or awareness (number)
  - Section 5 — Excluded from scope (number)
  - (blank row)
  - Documents not yet loaded but referenced (each on its own row,
    indented with two spaces)
  - (blank row)
  - Confidence (level and rationale)
  - (blank row)
  - Metadata-substance discrepancies flagged (if any, from the mapping)
  - (blank row)

  - (blank row)

    with two spaces)

Section labels ("Documents not yet loaded", "Confidence",
"Audit-objective considerations", "Gaps to flag") should be bold.

All cells: Arial 10pt, wrap text, top vertical, left horizontal.
Freeze pane at A2.
